Sigma Mini Hackathon

Below is the proposed outline for a recurring build-session (hackathon?). A critical piece to this group is the starting AND finishing of a project during the meeting.

0:00 - 0:30

This is our brainstorming time. It looks like this:

Students are given 4 post-it notes.

  • 3 are for project ideas. Ex:to-do list, mad-lib generator, etc...
  • 1 is for a project add-on. Ex: "that makes animal sounds"

Spend 3 minutes writing down ideas for projects you would like to see built

Each student then gets 3 minutes to eplain their ideas to the group as they place their post-its on the board/table (Keeping add ons hidden)

Once all ideas have been explained and put on display, the group goes through 3 rounds of voting to wittle down the ideas to one working project.

  1. Each person gets two votes (add-ons are not included in this round). At the end of this round of voting, any ideas without at least 2 votes are removed.
  2. Each person get 1 vote. Idea with the most votes wins. If there is a tie, vote again amoungst the tied ideas.
  3. Add-ons are voted on. 1 vote per person. Most votes wins. Tie breakers as necessary.

This seems like a really dumb system, oh well

Combine project and add-on, this is what we're building

0:30 - 1:00

Scoping Time

Make quick wireframes and write scope document

Assign rolls to folks

1:00 - 2:00

First Build Hour

Build stuff

2:00 - 2:15

Check in

Take a step back, check in with the group. What road blocks are we hitting. What do we have done so far?

All merge to master

2:15 - 3:15

Second Build Hour

Keep Building

3:15 - 3:30

Check in

Another step back. Check in with the group

All merge to master

3:30 - 4:30

Final Build Hour

Keep building!

4:30 - 4:45

Final merge

As a group, merge everything to master. We're finished!

4:45 - 5:00

Recap

What did we do well this time? What should we change going forward?
